Peptide Research Education

Build your foundation in peptide science

What Are Research Peptides?

Short chains of amino acids (typically 2-50 residues) used as tools in biochemical and pharmacological research. They serve as receptor ligands, enzyme substrates, and signaling molecules across virtually every field of biological science.

Understanding Peptide Purity

Purity ≥98% means fewer than 2% of the material consists of synthesis byproducts, truncated sequences, or degradation products. High purity is critical because impurities can produce confounding results, false signals, or cytotoxicity in sensitive assays.

Proper Storage & Handling

Lyophilized peptides should be stored at -20°C or below. Upon reconstitution (typically with bacteriostatic water), maintain at 2-8°C and use within 30 days. Protect from light, avoid freeze-thaw cycles, and maintain cold chain integrity during transport.

Reading Lab Results

HPLC (High-Performance Liquid Chromatography) separates peptide components to determine purity. Mass spectrometry confirms molecular identity by measuring exact molecular weight. Together, these verify both identity and purity of research peptides.
