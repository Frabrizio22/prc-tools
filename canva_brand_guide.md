## 4. Design Rules

### Logo Placement
- **Always** include the logo â€” small, unobtrusive
- **Preferred positions:** Top-left or bottom-left
- **Minimum clear space:** 20px around logo on all sides
- **Size:** Never more than 15% of the total design width
- **Color:** White version on dark backgrounds, navy version on light backgrounds
- **Never** stretch, rotate, or add effects to the logo

### Text Hierarchy (Most Important â†’ Least)
1. **Headline** â€” Biggest, boldest, white. The thing you want people to read first.
2. **Subheadline** â€” Smaller, gray or teal. Supports the headline.
3. **Body** â€” Regular weight, readable size. The details.
4. **CTA** â€” Stands out via color (blue button/text), not size.
5. **Disclaimer** â€” Smallest, most subdued. Always present, never prominent.

### The 3-Second Rule
Someone scrolling should understand your post in 3 seconds. If they can't:
- Too much text â†’ cut it
- Headline too small â†’ make it bigger
- Too many colors â†’ simplify

### Do's âœ…
- **DO** use lots of whitespace (or "dark space" on navy backgrounds)
- **DO** stick to 2â€“3 colors per design max
- **DO** keep text to 7 words or fewer per headline
- **DO** use the same fonts every single time
- **DO** include "For Research Use Only" on every product-related post
- **DO** use high-quality stock photos (lab equipment, molecular structures, clean environments)
- **DO** maintain consistent margins (40px for square posts, 50px for stories)

### Don'ts âŒ
- **DON'T** use more than 3 fonts in one design (you have 3, stick to them)
- **DON'T** put text over busy image areas without a dark overlay
- **DON'T** use bright/neon colors â€” you're a science brand, not a nightclub
- **DON'T** center-align body text longer than 2 lines (left-align instead)
- **DON'T** use clip art, cartoon graphics, or anything that looks "homemade"
- **DON'T** make the disclaimer large or prominent â€” but never skip it
- **DON'T** use red as an accent color (reads as "danger/warning" in science context)
- **DON'T** put important text in the outer 40px of any design (gets cropped)

### Photo & Background Style Guide

**Stock imagery that works:**
- Clean laboratory environments (beakers, vials, microscopes)
- Molecular structure visualizations (3D renders)
- Close-ups of research equipment
- Abstract science textures (DNA helixes, peptide chains)
- Minimalist flat-lay of vials on clean surfaces
- Blue/teal-tinted scientific photography

**Stock imagery to AVOID:**
- People injecting anything
- Bodybuilders or fitness imagery (you're research, not fitness)
- Cartoon/illustration style science graphics
- Overly stock-photo-looking "scientist smiling at camera"
- Anything medical/clinical that implies human use

**Where to find good free stock photos:**
- Unsplash.com â†’ search "laboratory," "molecular," "science research"
- Pexels.com â†’ search "peptide," "chemistry," "biotech"
- Canva's built-in stock library â†’ search same terms

### Disclaimer Placement
- **Text:** "For Research Use Only" (minimum)
- **Position:** Bottom of every product-related design
- **Style:** Inter Regular, smallest readable size (16â€“20px), `#8E99A4` on dark, `#94A3B8` on light
- **Never skip it** on product posts, promos, or anything showing a product name/price
- **Educational posts** about general science topics can skip it (but including it doesn't hurt)
