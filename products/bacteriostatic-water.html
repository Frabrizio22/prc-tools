What is Bacteriostatic Water?

Bacteriostatic water is sterile, non-pyrogenic water for injection containing 0.9% benzyl alcohol as a bacteriostatic preservative. The benzyl alcohol inhibits bacterial growth in the solution, allowing for multiple withdrawals from the same vial under sterile conditions.

Common Laboratory Uses

  • Peptide Reconstitution — Reconstituting lyophilized peptides for in vitro research applications
  • Laboratory Dilution — Preparing research compound solutions to desired concentrations
  • Multiple Withdrawals — Benzyl alcohol allows sterile multi-dose use over extended periods

Storage and Handling

Store at room temperature (20-25°C / 68-77°F). Once opened, the vial can be used for multiple withdrawals if proper sterile technique is maintained. Do not use if the solution is cloudy or contains particulates. For laboratory research purposes only.

Research Use Only: This product is intended for laboratory and research use only. Not for human or veterinary use. Bacteriostatic water must be handled using proper sterile technique in controlled research environments.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I reconstitute peptides with bacteriostatic water?

Draw the appropriate volume of bacteriostatic water using a sterile syringe, inject it gently down the side of the peptide vial (not directly onto the powder), and allow it to dissolve naturally without shaking. Swirl gently if needed. Store reconstituted peptides refrigerated (2-8°C) for in vitro use.

What's the difference between bacteriostatic water and sterile water?

Bacteriostatic water contains 0.9% benzyl alcohol as a preservative, which inhibits bacterial growth and allows for multiple withdrawals from the same vial. Sterile water contains no preservative and is intended for single-use only.

How long does an opened vial last?

When handled with proper sterile technique, bacteriostatic water can be used for multiple withdrawals over 28 days after first opening. Always inspect for cloudiness or particulates before each use.
