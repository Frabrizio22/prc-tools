AOD-9604 Research Guide: The Fragment That Targets Fat

March 12, 2026 · 8 min read
If you want the fat-burning effects of growth hormone without touching blood sugar or insulin sensitivity, AOD-9604 is the peptide that was designed to do exactly that. It's a modified fragment of human growth hormone's C-terminal region—specifically, the last 15 amino acids of the 191-amino-acid hGH molecule. Here's what makes this fragment interesting for metabolic research.

What is AOD-9604?

AOD-9604 (Advanced Obesity Drug 9604) is a synthetic peptide fragment derived from amino acids 176-191 of human growth hormone. Researchers at Monash University modified this fragment by adding a tyrosine at the N-terminus to stabilize the structure—that modification is what makes AOD-9604 different from the unmodified hGH fragment.

The molecule was specifically designed to retain growth hormone's lipolytic (fat-breaking) effects while eliminating its insulin-antagonistic effects. In other words: burn fat without messing with blood sugar.

Key insight: AOD-9604 doesn't bind to growth hormone receptors. That's the whole point. It works through a different mechanism that targets fat cells directly, which is why it doesn't cause the insulin resistance or blood sugar issues you see with full hGH.

The Mechanism: How AOD-9604 Works

AOD-9604's mechanism is built around two actions:

1. Lipolysis (Fat Breakdown)

The peptide stimulates lipolysis—the breakdown of stored triglycerides in adipocytes (fat cells) into free fatty acids and glycerol. This happens through activation of hormone-sensitive lipase, the enzyme that actually splits apart stored fat.

Research shows AOD-9604 increases lipolytic activity in adipose tissue without requiring growth hormone receptor activation. That's unusual. Most peptides that affect fat metabolism work through receptor pathways; AOD-9604 appears to work more directly on fat cells themselves.

2. Inhibition of Lipogenesis (Fat Storage)

The second mechanism: AOD-9604 reduces the conversion of non-fat food materials (like carbohydrates) into body fat. It inhibits lipogenesis by interfering with the enzymes that synthesize new fat molecules.

So you get a dual effect: more fat breakdown, less fat storage. That's the theoretical basis for why this fragment became an obesity research target.

Research Data: What Studies Show

Early Monash Studies (1990s-2000s)

The original research from Monash University showed AOD-9604 increased fat loss in obese animal models without affecting lean body mass or glucose metabolism. In rodent studies, treated subjects showed:

  • Reduced body weight compared to controls
  • Decreased fat mass (measured via DEXA scan)
  • No change in blood glucose or insulin levels
  • No cartilage or bone growth (unlike full hGH)

Human Trials

Metabolic Pharmaceuticals ran Phase II clinical trials in the early 2000s. The results were... mixed.

A 2007 study published in Diabetes Technology & Therapeutics followed 300+ obese adults over 12 weeks. The AOD-9604 group showed statistically greater weight loss than placebo in some dosing cohorts—but the effect was modest (around 2.6kg vs 0.8kg placebo at the highest dose).

The bigger issue: variability. Not everyone responded equally, and the FDA ultimately didn't approve it as an obesity drug because the efficacy wasn't consistent enough across trial populations. That's why you don't see AOD-9604 prescribed as a pharmaceutical—it exists purely in the research space now.

The safety profile was clean: No adverse effects on glucose tolerance, no insulin resistance, no IGF-1 elevation. That's the advantage of a fragment that doesn't touch growth hormone receptors.

Why Researchers Use AOD-9604

Despite not becoming an FDA-approved drug, AOD-9604 remains valuable in metabolic research for several reasons:

Selective Lipolysis Research

If you're studying fat metabolism pathways that don't involve growth hormone receptor activation, AOD-9604 is a useful tool. It lets you isolate lipolytic effects without the confounding variables of full hGH (IGF-1 elevation, insulin resistance, cartilage growth, etc.).

Combination Studies

Some researchers use AOD-9604 in combination protocols—stacking it with other metabolic peptides or compounds to study synergistic effects on adipose tissue. The clean side-effect profile makes it easier to attribute outcomes to specific mechanisms.

Body Composition Research

Because AOD-9604 targets fat mass without affecting lean mass or bone density, it's useful in studies examining body composition changes independent of muscle or skeletal effects.

AOD-9604 vs Other Fat-Loss Peptides

AOD-9604 vs Full hGH

  • hGH: Broad anabolic effects, increases IGF-1, builds muscle, can cause insulin resistance at high doses, affects cartilage/bone
  • AOD-9604: Targeted lipolysis only, no IGF-1 elevation, no insulin effects, no growth effects

AOD-9604 vs GLP-1 Agonists (Semaglutide, Tirzepatide)

  • GLP-1s: Work through appetite suppression + insulin regulation, affect gut motility, cause nausea in many users
  • AOD-9604: Targets fat cells directly, doesn't suppress appetite, no GI side effects, doesn't touch insulin pathways

Completely different mechanisms. GLP-1s reduce caloric intake; AOD-9604 (theoretically) increases fat oxidation without reducing food intake.

AOD-9604 vs MOTS-C

  • MOTS-C: Mitochondrial peptide, improves insulin sensitivity, enhances glucose metabolism
  • AOD-9604: Direct lipolytic effect, no insulin/glucose interaction

MOTS-C is metabolic regulation; AOD-9604 is targeted fat breakdown.

Purity and Sourcing Considerations

Like all research peptides, purity matters. AOD-9604 should be ≥98% pure via HPLC analysis. Lower purity means more degradation products or synthesis byproducts—things that can confound research results.

Look for:

  • HPLC purity ≥98%: Verified by third-party testing
  • Lyophilized (freeze-dried) format: More stable than liquid formulations
  • Certificate of Analysis (COA): Should include purity percentage, molecular weight confirmation, and sterility testing
Research Use Only: AOD-9604 is not FDA-approved for human consumption or therapeutic use. All information provided is for educational purposes related to in vitro research only.

Storage and Stability

AOD-9604 peptides should be stored at:

  • Lyophilized (powder): -20°C (freezer), protected from light. Stable for 12-24 months.
  • Reconstituted (liquid): 2-8°C (refrigerator), use within 14 days for optimal stability.

Peptides degrade faster once reconstituted. If you're running multi-week protocols, consider using smaller vials to minimize freeze-thaw cycles.

Bottom Line

AOD-9604 is a targeted lipolytic fragment designed to deliver growth hormone's fat-burning effects without touching insulin, IGF-1, or skeletal growth. The human trial data was promising but inconsistent—enough to be interesting for research, not enough to become an FDA-approved drug.

If you're studying fat metabolism pathways independent of growth hormone receptor activation, AOD-9604 gives you a clean tool to isolate lipolytic mechanisms. That's its value in metabolic research—and why it's still used despite not becoming a pharmaceutical product.
